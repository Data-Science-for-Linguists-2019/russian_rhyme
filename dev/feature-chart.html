Based on http://linguistics.byu.edu/faculty/eddingtond/223/distinctive%20features%20chart.pdf, which is not available at its original address as of 2019-06-28, but can be retrieved from https://web.archive.org/web/20160708164639/http://linguistics.byu.edu/faculty/eddingtond/223/distinctive%20features%20chart.pdf.

For consonants, position is fully defined by anterior and coronal, so back, high, and low are omitted. Round and strident are omitted because they are not distinctive. Palatalized has been added (includes true palatal /j/). Affricates are considered +continuant, although they are not listed that way in the source.

For vowels, position is fully defined by high, low, and back, so round and ATR are omitted. Stressed and unstressed vowels have the same distinctive features; stress is represented on a different level.

Values have been supplied for all features, even when they are not actually distinctive for particular segments (e.g., all fricatives have been classified as –delayed release).
